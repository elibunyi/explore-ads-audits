Executive Summary

The $611 CPA is the product of four compounding failures, not a single cause. This account spends money well but converts almost none of it — the root is structural, not budgetary. Max Clicks with no CPC cap gives Google a blank check; a single monolithic ad group with 45 mixed-intent keywords prevents any relevance signal from reaching the right users; search term waste bleeds roughly 30–40% of budget into competitor brands, wrong materials, DIY intent, and informational queries; and a catastrophic landing page conversion rate (~0.4% on the homepage — 246 clicks, 1 conversion) means even the relevant traffic isn't closing. Beaufort is also a genuinely small market — Beaufort County has ~200K residents, limiting addressable volume. A full reboot is warranted. Incremental fixes will not solve this.

Root Cause Analysis — Why $611 CPA?

  • Bidding strategy (PRIMARY): Max Clicks (TARGET_SPEND) with zero CPC cap — Google is spending up to $8–12/click on irrelevant competitor and informational queries with no guardrail. The budget is exhausted chasing clicks, not leads. No smart bidding signal exists (only 2 conversions in 30 days, far below the 30+ needed for tCPA to function).
  • Single ad group with 45 mixed-intent keywords: All 45 keywords — from "fiberglass pool installer" to "pool contractors" to "local pool" — live in one ad group called "Ad group 1." Google cannot differentiate which terms are high vs. low intent. QS suffers across the board. Multiple keywords show QS=0, meaning they are actively suppressed or achieving below-minimum quality thresholds.
  • Search term waste (~30–40% of spend): Competitor brands (Latham Pools $35, Blue Haven $10, Blue Haven fiberglass $4.49) — despite Latham being a negative now, the damage is evidenced in historical data. Wrong-material queries (vinyl inground pool $6.06). Wrong-product queries (container pool $8.39, cowboy pool $6.43). Informational/no-intent (inground pool cost $12.70, national pool companies $9.94, how to replaster $4.94). Active negatives only recently added are still visible in historical spend totals.
  • Landing page failure: Homepage (leisurepoolsbeaufort.com) received 246 clicks and generated only 1 conversion — a 0.4% CVR. Fleet average for LP dealers in relevant markets runs 1.5–3%. Even with perfect targeting, this CVR destroys the CPA math. Sitelink sub-pages (pool designs, colors, brochure, contact) received minimal traffic and zero conversions.
  • Market size constraint: Beaufort SC is a small coastal market. Beaufort County population ~200K. Pool ownership intent queries in this DMA are limited by volume — even best-case IS expansion won't generate the conversion volume to drive CPA down without fixing CVR and bid strategy first.
  • Rank lost IS (49.9%): Nearly half of eligible impressions are lost to rank — not budget. This means Quality Score and/or bid is suppressing the account mid-auction. With Max Clicks and poor QS on many keywords, Google deprioritizes this account even when budget is available. Budget lost IS (18.6%) is secondary.

Campaign Reboot Assessment

Recommendation: FULL REBOOT WARRANTED

The current structure cannot be patched into performance. The single ad group architecture, Max Clicks with no cap, and accumulated quality signals from 45 mixed-intent keywords make incremental improvement difficult and slow. A reboot gives a clean slate with:

  • 3–4 tightly themed ad groups: [Fiberglass Pool Installation], [Inground Pool Builders], [Pool Company + Local], [Pool Price + Quote]
  • Switch to Manual CPC at ~$3.50–4.50 max with strong negatives, then graduate to tCPA after 30+ conversions
  • New RSAs per ad group with tightly matched headlines — current RSA has "Pool Installation Prices" and "Family-Friendly Fun" competing in the same ad, which tanks Ad Strength relevance signals (currently rated 6/10)
  • Landing page: Request dealer add a dedicated "Get a Quote" landing page with form above the fold — homepage CVR of 0.4% is the single biggest lever available
  • Add 15–20 high-specificity negatives for competitor brands not yet blocked: Blue Haven, national pool companies, replaster, vinyl inground
  • Budget reallocation: $32.25/day is appropriate for the market — do not increase until CVR is fixed

Note: Reboot prep time ~2–3 hours. Expected improvement timeline: 30–45 days to see CPA improvement as new structure accumulates QS signals and conversion data.
