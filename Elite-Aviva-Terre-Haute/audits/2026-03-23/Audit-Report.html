Max Clicks Bidding With $250 CPC Cap — Ineffective Strategy
The campaign uses Target Spend (Max Clicks) with a $250 CPC cap — a cap so high it functions as no cap at all. Max Clicks is a volume-oriented strategy that optimizes for clicks without regard to conversion likelihood. For a low-volume market with high ticket products:
— Max Clicks can drive irrelevant clicks to exhaust budget
— No conversion signal is fed back to the bidding algorithm
— With only 2 conversions in 30 days, the account does not have sufficient conversion data for Smart Bidding (Target CPA requires ~50 conv/month baseline)

However: switching to Target CPA is not currently viable given conversion volume. The correct path is to fix structural issues first (negatives, extensions, ad groups), accumulate conversion data, then re-evaluate bidding strategy at 60-day review.
Reduce Max CPC cap from $250 to $15–20 as a guardrail against outlier CPCs. Do not switch bidding strategy until conversion volume improves. Revisit at 60-day post-rebuild review.
