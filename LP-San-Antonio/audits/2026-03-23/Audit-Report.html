Priority Action Items

  • P1

    Pricing Page Zero-Conversion Sink — $415/mo, 105 clicks, 0 conversions

    The /pool-pricing/ landing page received 105 clicks and $415 in spend with zero conversions. This is the #3 highest-spend URL in the account. Either the page has no CTA/form above the fold, or pricing discourages form fills. Audit the page and either add a lead capture form, redirect to the designs page, or exclude the pricing page as a destination. The "Fiberglass Pools Cost/Pricing" ad group is the primary source.

  • P1

    "Fiberglass Pools Cost/Pricing" Ad Group — $484 spend, 121 clicks, 0 conversions

    This ad group is the account's worst performer by CPL. $484 spent, not a single conversion. Keywords in this group target informational pricing queries — users researching cost, not ready to buy. Either pause this ad group, or restructure it to send traffic to a page with a strong lead capture mechanism (e.g., "Get Pricing Sent to You" form). At current trajectory this is ~$175/week in wasted budget.

  • P1

    Pool Builder / Contractor Campaign — 20.8% Impression Share, 61.8% Rank-Lost IS

    The "Pool Builder / Contractor" campaign is critically underserving — winning only 1 in 5 auctions due to rank (not budget). 61.8% of lost impressions are rank-lost, meaning the $15 CPC cap is too low for competitive builder/contractor queries. The campaign spent $1,325 at $6.05 avg CPC with 7 conversions — the keyword "swimming pool builder" [phrase] is the top spender. Raise the CPC ceiling from $15 → $25 or switch to Target CPA to let Google bid to conversion intent. The IS loss on this campaign is leaving real leads on the table.

  • P1

    Competitor & Irrelevant Brand Terms Active in Search Traffic

    Multiple competitor and off-target branded search terms are triggering ads: "aqua pools san antonio" ($6.87), "aqua pools new braunfels" ($6.26), "gary pools san antonio" ($7.28), "leisure pools new braunfels" (appearing 3x, $18+$14+$14 = $46+ total), and others. "Leisure Pools New Braunfels" is a different dealer — this is competitor dealer leakage. Add these as exact-match negatives immediately. Also: "pila pool" ($16, already added as exact keyword — likely irrelevant intent), "container pools texas" ($9, added as keyword), "stock tank pools" variants should be excluded.

  • P2

    Photo Gallery Pages Absorbing $300/mo — 61 Clicks, 0 Conversions

    Two photo gallery page entries ($182k + $118k micros = ~$300 combined) account for 61 clicks and zero conversions. This may be informational traffic or the gallery page may not have a strong CTA. Consider adding a persistent "Get Your Free Estimate" sticky CTA to the gallery page, or exclude the photo gallery from ad destinations and send all traffic to the designs/estimate page instead.

  • P2

    Shared Negative List Has Gaps — Specific Pool Competitors Not Covered

    The "Master Exclusion List 3.18" (162 terms) covers general negative categories (jobs, DIY, inflatable, etc.) well. However, it does not block specific local competitor names (Aqua Pools, Gary Pools, Elegant Pools, etc.) or Leisure Pools regional dealers (New Braunfels, Austin, DFW). Add campaign-level or list-level negatives for known local San Antonio competitors and sister Leisure Pools dealers.

  • P2

    No Business Name or Logo Asset Configured

    Campaign assets include sitelinks (field_type 13), callouts (field_type 11), a call asset (field_type 16), and a field_type 27 asset. No business name (field_type 39) or logo assets (field_type 38) are present. These are now standard for brand presence in RSA serving. Add "Leisure Pools San Antonio" as a business name asset and upload the Leisure Pools logo to improve ad presentation.

  • P3

    The "Service Areas" sitelink description reads "New Braunfels, Blanco, & more." These are correct service areas for this dealer, but worth confirming the dealer actively serves all referenced locations to avoid ad disapproval or user frustration.

  • P3
