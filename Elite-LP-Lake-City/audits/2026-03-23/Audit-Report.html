Priority Findings
P1
Single Ad Group — All 12 Keywords Undifferentiated
Every keyword (inground pools, fiberglass pools, pool builder, pool installation, leisure pools, etc.) lives in one ad group "Ad group 1" with a single RSA. There is no thematic segmentation, no ability to control bids by intent tier, and no custom ad copy per theme. This is the root cause of the high CPA — a generic ad serves all query types regardless of buyer intent.
P1
Budget Too Low for Max Clicks — 16.2% IS Lost to Budget
The account is on Maximize Clicks (bidding strategy type 9 = TARGET_SPEND) with a daily budget of just $16.12/day (~$484/mo). This is causing the account to miss ~16% of eligible impressions due to budget exhaustion. For a small market like Lake City, this may be near-optimal, but the algorithm is throttling delivery. Combined with 34.3% IS lost to rank, the account is only capturing 49.5% of available impression share.
P1
Homepage LP Dominates Spend ($436) — Poor Conversion Architecture
The homepage (leisurepoolslakecity.com) received $435.54 of the $525.66 total spend — 82.9% — with only 1 conversion at a $435 CPA. The dedicated brochure page received $64 and 1 conversion ($64 CPA). The /fiberglass-pool-designs/ and /free-estimate/ pages received traffic from sitelinks but zero conversions despite $21 in spend. The account is funneling almost all spend into the weakest converting LP.
P1
RSA Headlines Unreadable via API — Manual Review Required
The ad_group_ad query returned a serialization error on the headlines/descriptions composite fields. The ad copy cannot be verified through the API. A manual UI review is required to confirm: (1) no brand stuffing, (2) adequate message match to keywords, (3) at least 1 pinned headline with a location-specific call-out (Lake City, FL).

Small Market Analysis — Is Budget Appropriate?
Direct answer: The budget is roughly appropriate for the market size, but the structure is making it perform poorly. Lake City FL is a small, rural market. At $484/mo, this account will never generate high conversion volume — the goal should be efficiency, not scale.
Factor Assessment
Market Population (Columbia County, FL) ~70,000 county / ~12,500 city — low pool-buying population
Pool Installation Demand Limited — rural Florida, modest income market
Search Volume Proxy 1,105 impressions / 30 days = ~37 impressions/day at 49.5% IS → ~75 total market impressions/day. Very low.
Budget Ceiling Estimate Even at 100% IS, the account would capture ~75 impressions/day. At ~11% CTR = ~8 clicks/day = $34/day max efficient spend. Current $16/day is about 47% of theoretical ceiling.
Ideal Budget Range $18–25/day ($540–$750/mo). Beyond $25/day, returns diminish in this market.
CPA Expectation at Optimum With correct LP, bid strategy, and negatives: $80–$120 realistic CPA target. Current $263 is 2–3x too high.
Conclusion: The underperformance is structural, not a budget problem. The account is wasting ~25% of its tiny budget on irrelevant terms, then funneling remaining traffic to the wrong landing page (homepage). Fix the structure first; then consider a modest budget increase to $20–22/day.
