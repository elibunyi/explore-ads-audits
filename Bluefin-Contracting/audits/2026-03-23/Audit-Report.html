P1 — Critical Issues
P1
Target Spend — No CPC Cap Set (cpc_bid_ceiling_micros = 0)
Max Clicks with no ceiling is a budget bleed risk at this spend level.
▼

Campaign "Bluefin Contracting & Design Campaign" is using Target Spend (Max Clicks, type=9) bidding — the correct strategy for an account with only 7 conversions in 30 days. However, cpc_bid_ceiling_micros = 0, meaning there is no upper bound on what Google can bid per click.

Without a cap, Smart Bidding can push individual clicks to $8–$15+ on broad-intent queries where a $3–$5 click limit would be appropriate for this market size and budget. At $16.12/day, even 2–3 blown clicks wipes out the day's budget.

Recommended fix: Set cpc_bid_ceiling to $6–$8. This preserves Max Clicks volume while preventing runaway individual clicks. Revisit after 30 more days of data or once conversions reach 15–20/month.

P2 — Significant Issues
P2
Single Ad Group — No Intent Segmentation
All 13 keywords and 100% of traffic funnel through "Ad group 1." Brand, high-intent commercial, and generic queries compete in the same bucket.
▼

The entire campaign runs through one ad group: "Ad group 1" (ID: 179608256386). This creates several compounding problems:

  • Brand keywords ("bluefin contracting," "blue fin contracting") sit alongside generic commercial keywords — brand CTR inflates the overall group average, masking poor performance on non-brand terms.
  • Ad relevance and Quality Score cannot be optimized per intent tier — one RSA must serve every query from "pool builder" to "fiberglass pool companies" to brand terms.
  • 3 of 7 conversions come from brand queries; without segmentation, it's impossible to measure true commercial (non-brand) efficiency.
  • Budget allocation cannot be controlled between brand and commercial intent — brand clicks burn commercial budget.

Recommended fix: Split into at minimum 3 ad groups: (1) Brand only — "bluefin contracting," "blue fin contracting" — or ideally a separate brand campaign; (2) High-intent commercial — "pool builder," "pool contractor," "pool installation," "pool builders near me"; (3) Product-specific — "fiberglass pool companies," "fiberglass inground swimming pool."
