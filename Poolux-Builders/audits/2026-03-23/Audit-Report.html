This is effectively instructing Google: "Only enter auctions where we can win for a penny." In the pool installation market, that means Google ignores virtually every real buyer query — which clears $2–$8 in CPC — and instead serves ads on national generic price-research queries where nobody else is bidding. The result: 1,132 clicks from people researching cocktail pools in Florida, plunge pool pricing nationally, and swimming pools in New York, none of whom are in Poolux's Arizona service area. The $18/day budget is not the problem — the penny bid ceiling is.
