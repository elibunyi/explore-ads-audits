The unusually high CTR is driven by queries where Cajun Pools' ad appears to directly answer an informational question — users click expecting educational content or pricing info, not to request a pool quote. Examples:

• "cocktail pool" — 85% CTR (13 clicks, $11.70 spend, 0 conversions)
• "small yard cocktail pool" — 84.9% CTR (62 clicks, $52.65 spend, 0 conversions)
• "fiberglass pools with prices" — 85.7% CTR (6 clicks, $4.56 spend, 0 conversions)
• "pool sizes and prices" — 71.3% CTR (62 clicks, $45.67 spend, 0 conversions)

High CTR + zero conversion = classic intent mismatch pattern. Users who search "cocktail pool" are browsing options, not ready to call a contractor. The ad headline likely triggers the click; the landing page doesn't deliver conversion content for that intent.

CTR > 1.0 Data Anomaly on Several Terms

Multiple search terms show CTR values mathematically above 100% (e.g., "pool installation cost" CTR=1.5, "fiberglass pool kits for sale" CTR=2.0, "gunite pools" CTR=2.0). This is a known Google Ads reporting anomaly where impression counts don't align with click records in the search term view — particularly for low-impression terms. This is a data artifact, not fraud.
