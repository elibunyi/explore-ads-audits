Critical
No CPC Bid Cap on Maximize Clicks Strategy — Uncapped Spend Per Click

The campaign runs Maximize Clicks (TARGET_SPEND) with target_spend.cpc_bid_ceiling_micros = 0 — confirmed via API. Google has full discretion over how much to bid per click, with no upper boundary.

This is materially harmful for a pool lead account where the average click costs $6.89. Without a cap, Smart Bidding can and will pay $15–$30+ per click on broad matching scenarios, which compounds the damage from the zero-negative problem above — the system pays top dollar for irrelevant queries.

Action: Set CPC bid ceiling. Recommended range for this market: $8–$12 max CPC. This caps worst-case click cost while keeping the campaign competitive on quality signals.
🟠 High Severity Findings
High
Single Ad Group Architecture — All 18 Keywords Share One Ad Group

The entire account runs through one campaign → one ad group ("Ad group 1") → 18 keywords. This is a structurally suboptimal setup for a pool dealer where intent varies significantly across query types.

The current mix in a single ad group includes: brand queries (backyard pool and patio), generic pool queries (backyard pool), service-specific queries (swimming pool installation), and product-specific queries (fiberglass pools). These have different CTR expectations and ideal ad copy — forced to compete for the same RSA.

Practical impact: the single RSA must balance messaging for someone searching "backyard pool" (awareness stage) vs. "pool installation near me" (high intent, ready-to-buy). Relevance suffers on both ends.

Action (Medium-Term): Restructure into minimum 3 ad groups: (1) Brand/Named, (2) Inground/Fiberglass Install-Intent, (3) General/Awareness. Each with dedicated ad copy matching the intent tier.
High
3 Keywords with Quality Score = 0 (Not Enough Data)

Three keywords show QS=0, indicating insufficient data for scoring: swimming pool contractors (QS 0, $327 spend, 47 clicks, 1.5 conv), swimming pool designs (QS 0, $103 spend, 15 clicks), and fiberglass pool companies (QS 0, 0 impr). Two of these are actively spending despite no formal QS.

swimming pool contractors is the second-highest spend keyword at $327 with a QS of 0. This is a high-risk position — Google cannot assess relevance, which can inflate CPCs and hurt Ad Rank. The keyword "contractors" also signals a searcher looking for a contractor, not necessarily a pool product retailer — potentially misaligned intent.

Action: Review swimming pool contractors — if CTR and conversion rate don't improve with better ad copy tailored to it, consider pausing in favor of higher-QS alternatives. fiberglass pool companies has zero impressions — already inactive in practice, low priority.

(3) If QS does not improve after 2 weeks of negative filtering, evaluate replacing with tighter alternatives: "inground backyard pool" or "backyard fiberglass pool".
Medium
RSA Ad Strength: "Poor" (6/10) — Ad Copy Optimization Needed

The single RSA in "Ad group 1" (ad ID: 750430631683) has an ad strength score of 6, which maps to "Poor" on Google's scale. Policy approval status is approved (4). The RSA does not include pinned path fields that would indicate location-specific tailoring.

Per audit parameters, RSA headline/description content cannot be read via the API — these findings are based on the ad strength signal only. A "Poor" score at this spend level means Google is limiting auction competitiveness and likely suppressing impression share.

Action: Log into the account UI and review RSA asset pinning and headline coverage. Common causes of "Poor" strength: too few headlines, similar/duplicate headlines, no inclusion of top keywords in headlines. Target "Good" or "Excellent" strength. Add local signals: "Bloomington IL Pool Builder", "Central Illinois Fiberglass Pools".
Medium
7 Zero-Impression Keywords — Dead Weight in Single Ad Group

7 of 18 keywords have zero impressions in the 30-day window: fiberglass swimming pool, fiberglass pool companies, pool builder near me, pool contractor near me, fiberglass inground pool, inground fiberglass pool, and pool builders near me (5 clicks, $0 reported).

Zero-impression keywords in a single-ad-group structure dilute the ad group's focus signal. More importantly, some of these are high-intent keywords (pool builder near me, pool contractor near me) that should be generating volume but aren't — suggesting either low search volume in the geo, bid suppression, or QS issues.

Action: Audit the zero-impression high-intent keywords for bid sufficiency and QS. "Pool builder near me" and "pool contractor near me" are strong local-intent terms that shouldn't be invisible — investigate and potentially restructure into a dedicated "Near Me" ad group with tighter, location-focused copy.
