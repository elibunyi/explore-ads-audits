Critical Findings 3 Issues
P1 Single Ad Group — All Traffic Funneled Through "Pool Builder"

The entire account runs through one ad group with 25 keywords spanning intent ranges from "pool contractor" ($6.54 CPC, QS 5) to "pool company Colorado" ($6.63 CPC, QS 0). Mixing high-intent contractor queries with lower-intent "pool company" and "fiberglass pool" queries in a single ad group forces one RSA to serve all query types. QS suffers because headlines cannot simultaneously be highly relevant to contractor queries AND pool cost queries AND specific geo queries. This is the primary driver of 36.4% IS lost to rank.

Recommended fix: Split into at least 3 themed ad groups — (1) Contractor/Builder queries, (2) Pool type/material queries ("fiberglass pool Colorado"), (3) Cost/quote queries ("pool cost," "pool price"). Each group gets its own RSA with matched headlines.
P1 36.4% Impression Share Lost to Rank — QS-Driven, Not Bid-Driven

Campaign is on Max Clicks (TARGET_SPEND) with no CPC cap (cpc_bid_ceiling_micros = 0), meaning Google can bid any amount per click. Despite uncapped bidding, 36.4% of eligible auctions are being lost to rank — confirming the problem is Quality Score, not bid floor. "pool company Colorado" has QS 0 (unrated/not enough data but no conversions on $199 spend). "pool contractor Colorado" has QS 3 on $296 spend — the geo-modified variant of the top keyword is significantly underperforming the unmodified "pool contractor" (QS 5).

Recommended fix: Ad group segmentation (above) is the primary lever. Additionally, review RSA pinning — if headlines are pinned to positions that don't align with query intent, unpin and let Google optimize. Ensure landing page copy mentions Colorado and Front Range prominently to improve landing page QS component.
