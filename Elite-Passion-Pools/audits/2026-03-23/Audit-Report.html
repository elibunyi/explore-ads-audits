Cincinnati Performance Drop — Root Cause Analysis

Root Cause: Max Conversions Re-Learning After 3/18 Sitelink Changes

Change event data confirms all 3 campaigns had sitelink structure edits on 2026-03-18 at 20:51, 20:55, and 20:58 EST. Each batch modified both asset records (resource_type=14) and campaign_asset records (resource_type=16). Editor: eli.bunyi@exploreindustries.com via Google Ads UI.

Cincinnati's campaign (22480126961) was edited first at 20:51 EST on 3/18. Its 43.1% impression share and $177/conv CPA vs NKY's 58.4% share / $116/conv indicate the Max Conversions algorithm entered a re-learning period post-sitelink change that it has not fully exited.

Why this happens:

  • Max Conversions uses ad strength signals including asset combinations. A significant sitelink change (new text, added/removed descriptions) invalidates the auction model's learned asset performance weights.
  • The algorithm must re-test new asset combinations before bidding aggressively — this manifests as lower IS and higher CPA during the 2-3 week learning window.
  • Cincinnati already had the lowest impression share (43.1%) and fewest conversions (13) before this analysis; the 3/18 change likely pushed it from marginal to clearly underperforming.
  • Dayton and NKY were also edited but may have had stronger conversion history to absorb the disruption.

Recommendation: Allow 1-2 more weeks before making additional structural changes to Cincinnati. If CPA hasn't improved to ≤$160 by 4/7, consider a manual CPC test on 2-3 top keywords to diagnose whether the bid strategy or the keywords are the issue.
